About Repro

Owner-side restaurant builders. 30 years. New England top to bottom.

Repro is a small, named shop — not a regional CM — and that's the point. When you call, Mark answers. When the framing's up, Nick's on site. When the inspector walks in, Chris has the paperwork ready.

01 / The shop

Founded in Peabody, built around restaurants.

Repro Construction Services has been building in New England for roughly thirty years out of 525 Lowell Street in Peabody, Massachusetts. Over 200 projects, around $7M in annual revenue, and a brand portfolio that's narrowed deliberately over time toward one lane: QSR and fast-casual restaurants. Inspire Brands. Dunkin'. Jersey Mike's. The Works Cafe. Wayback Burger. Yella on the Water. Zo Greek Cuisine. We've worked the others too — residential, multi-unit, light commercial — but restaurants are the strategic lane.

The geography is Massachusetts, New Hampshire, and Rhode Island, with selective work in the rest of New England when the project warrants it. We don't chase national rollouts. We grow with operators who already know us, and we earn the next franchisee through the last one.

03 / The principle

We manage your money like it's ours.

Owner-side accountability isn't a slogan here; it's the operating model. A regional CM hands you off to a project manager you'll forget by month three. We don't. The owner of the shop is the owner you talk to, on every project, every week. Decisions don't escalate — they happen on the call.

That changes the math on a job. Change orders get debated before they're written, not after. Vendor pricing gets pressure-tested against what we know the trade should bid. The schedule gets defended because the schedule is somebody's open-date, somebody's payroll, somebody's royalty clock. When we say "we manage your money like it's ours," what we mean is: the person whose name is on the company is the person making the call on what gets cut and what gets held. That's the trade. That's the whole pitch.
