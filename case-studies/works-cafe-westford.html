The Challenge

A brand audit you can spot from the door.

The Works Cafe is a New England original. Founded in 1988 in Hanover, NH, the brand has spent thirty-plus years building a reputation on thoughtfully sourced food — local cage-free eggs, humanely raised antibiotic-free meats, NY-style bagels baked in-store, organic ancient grains, locally-made GMO-free wraps. Everything in the dining room — wood, light, signage, the bake case at the counter — has to back that story up the second a guest walks in.

Westford was the brand's push deeper into Massachusetts. A high-visibility Route 110 spot, a competitive breakfast and lunch market, and a community that knew the brand by reputation before they ever opened the doors. Richard French and his team needed a builder who could deliver on three things at once:

  1. Hit the brand spec. Not "close enough." The Works' customers can spot a corner-cut bake case or the wrong wood finish from the door.
  2. Open on the target date. Every day a leased shell sits closed is rent paid, payroll burned, and royalties not flowing. There's no "we'll get it next week" in a cafe opening.
  3. Be reachable. Owners don't want to chase a project manager. They want to call the guy whose name is on the company and get a straight answer.

Richard had options. There are cheaper GCs in this market. He picked Repro.

The Build

How Westford came together.

A condensed timeline. Specific dates to be confirmed with Mark and Nick.

  1. 01

    Pre-construction

    Site walk with The Works' development team. Prototype book reconciled against the leased shell's existing MEP, ceiling height, and storefront constraints. Variance log produced and signed off before mobilization — no surprise change orders on the back end.

  2. 02

    Demo & rough-in

    Existing shell stripped to white box where required. Plumbing rough-in coordinated to the bakery line and the dishwashing pit. Electrical roughed for the bake case, espresso bar, hood, and POS.

  3. 03

    Mechanical & hood

    Kitchen ventilation, make-up air, and grease management installed to brand spec and to Westford's building department requirements. Final balance documented for the operator's records.

  4. 04

    Bakery line & counter

    The visible heart of any Works Cafe. Bake case, NY-style bagel display, beverage station, and order/pickup counter built to the prototype drawing — the right wood, the right sightlines, the right shadow gaps.

  5. 05

    Dining room & exterior

    Seating, lighting, finishes, restrooms, exterior signage, and storefront detail. The "I know this brand the second I see it" moment, executed once.

  6. 06

    Inspections, punch & turnover

    Health, building, and fire walked once each. Punch list closed inside {{PUNCH_CLOSE_DAYS}} days. Operator handed a clean store with documentation, warranty packet, and our phone numbers — not a binder and a goodbye.
