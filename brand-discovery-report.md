## 2. Visual identity signals

- **Logo:** "Repro" wordmark in a sans-serif on a transparent PNG (`repro-trans.png`). Used everywhere on the site. Appears bold, simple, industrial. No tagline locked to the mark.
- **Color cues:** Heavy use of white backgrounds, dark text. Photographic content drives color. Icon set uses simple line-art (sketch, budget, building, page icons) — no defined accent palette is visible in the existing site.
- **Imagery:** Strong reliance on actual jobsite photography — Dunkin' interiors, Jersey Mike's, Wayback Burgers, Dave's Hot Chicken, exterior shells of multi-units (Nahant St Revere), excavator and site-work shots. This is a real strength — they have a deep photo library.
- **Typography on the site:** Divi-default heading hierarchy (`##`, `####`). Not a deliberate type system — just theme defaults.

**Gap:** No visible brand book, no defined color palette, no defined type system. This is a 2022-era WordPress build with default theme styling. There is no codified visual identity — the new site is a clean-sheet opportunity.

## 3. Voice & tone signals (from existing copy)

**Recurring phrases on site:**
- "Transforming a client's vision into reality" (tagline-adjacent — appears on home page)
- "Over 30 Years Experience"
- "Retail Remodel Specialists"
- "Innovative solutions to construction challenges"
- "First class work and on-time completion"
- "We strive to act in a way that makes the owner look good in the eyes of their customers"
- "Precise budgeting, diligent scheduling and comprehensive communication"
- "Fast track projects on a design-build basis"
- "14 Day Remodel" (proof-point used as a banner)

**Voice characteristics observed:**
- **Confident, experience-led** — leans hard on "30 years," credentials, OSHA certifications, project counts.
- **Process-fluent** — uses real construction terminology (CSL, HIC, value engineering, constructability reviews, pre-construction). Talks to owners and architects, not just homeowners.
- **Slightly stiff / B2B** — reads like a capabilities document, not a brand. Not warm or distinctive. Some typos ("knowklage") suggest the copy was written quickly and never tightened.
- **Owner-aligned** — repeatedly frames Repro as the owner's agent, protecting their money and reputation. This is a real differentiator versus competitors who sound transactional.

**What's missing from the voice today:**
- No emotional or human element (the team bios are warmer than the rest of the site — that warmth doesn't extend to the marketing copy).
- No customer voice / testimonials surfaced.
- No clear residential vs. commercial separation in tone — the site tries to serve both audiences with the same voice and loses focus.

## 8. Recommended brand-voice direction (draft for review)

Based on what's discoverable, here's a starter direction the guidelines could codify — flag what's wrong and we'll iterate:

- **Personality:** Veteran, hands-on, no-BS New England builder. Confident without bragging. The contractor an owner trusts to act like a partner, not a vendor.
- **Voice pillars:**
  1. *Owner-side, not vendor-side.* — "We manage your money like it's ours."
  2. *Speed is a feature.* — Fast-track, 14-day remodel proof points.
  3. *Verticals over generalism.* — Restaurant, retail, multi-unit, residential — lead with what they're known for.
  4. *Local and built to last.* — 30+ years, New England DNA, named team members with bios.
- **Tone shifts by audience:** Tighter and more numerical for QSR / commercial buyers; warmer and more visual for residential homeowners.
- **Words to use:** design-build, fast-track, on time, on budget, retail remodel, tenant improvement, owner's representative.
- **Words to avoid:** corporate stock phrases ("synergy," "leverage," "world-class"), generic "your trusted partner" filler, anything that sounds AI-templated.
