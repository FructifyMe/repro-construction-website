## Production guardrails

- **Mike drafts Wednesday blog posts.** Nick or Mark approves before publish. Target 800-1,400 words. Each one ends with the same one-line CTA: "If you're weighing a build in the next six months — start the four-field intake at reproconstruction.com."
- **Nick writes Monday LinkedIn posts.** Mike provides hook + outline, Nick rewrites in his voice. Posted from Nick's personal account, cross-posted to company page 2 hours later.
- **Friday is Nick's repurpose lane.** Nothing original needs to be created Friday — it's recycling Monday or Wednesday into a different format. This is the slot most GCs skip and the one that compounds.
- **Photo source:** Nick's LinkedIn library (scraped via Claude in Chrome). No stock construction imagery, ever.
- **Brand-mark usage:** Any post with a Dunkin', Jersey Mike's, Inspire Brands, or Works Cafe wordmark requires the brand-permission emails to be closed before publish. See `/Repro/brand-permission-emails.md` (root, franchisor-level) and `/Repro/marketing-ops/brand-permission-emails.md` (operator-level).
- **Hold a content review every 4 weeks.** Mark, Nick, Mike — 30 minutes — what landed, what didn't, what shifts.

---

## What this calendar deliberately does *not* include

- **Holiday-themed posts.** QSR construction is not a holiday-driven business. Skip them.
- **Industry-day posts** ("National Pizza Day," etc.). Wallpaper content.
- **Recycled stock-photo carousels.** Operators read past them in 0.3 seconds.
- **Generic SEO listicles** ("Top 10 things to look for in a contractor"). They don't rank against brand-specific intent and they water down the premium voice.
