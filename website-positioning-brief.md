## 3. What everyone says (avoid this)

Reading these sites back-to-back, a few phrases recur until they become wallpaper. **The new Repro site should avoid all of these:**

- "Full service construction management firm"
- "On time and on budget"
- "We bring decades of experience"
- "Our team of experienced professionals"
- "We work closely with our clients"
- "From concept to completion"
- "Quality, safety, and sustainability"
- "Innovative solutions"
- Generic capability lists ("preconstruction, construction, closeout")

Every competitor uses several of these. They cancel each other out. None of it persuades.

---

## 4. White space — what nobody owns

There are real lanes nobody in this set has claimed clearly:

1. **Speed as the headline promise.** Cornerstone and Groom mention fast-track in passing; nobody centers it. Repro literally has a *"14-day Dunkin' remodel"* proof point sitting on their current home page in a way that gets buried. Speed is a buyable, measurable, customer-relevant differentiator — and the QSR ops director who renews leases on a schedule cares about this above almost everything else.

2. **The "we act like the owner" frame.** Repro's existing copy says *"we make decisions in the owner's best interest and manage owner's funds as if they were our own."* Nobody else in this set says this. Cafco gets close with testimonials, but doesn't make it the explicit promise. **This is Repro's most distinctive voice asset and it is currently buried at the bottom of the home page.**

3. **The named individual operator at the head of the company.** Mark Geissler is real, accessible, named, with a 30-year track record. Groom and Connolly use family-history language but feel institutional. Mark could front the brand the way an independent builder fronts a chef-driven restaurant — and most QSR franchisees prefer working with the boss who answers his phone over a faceless CM firm.

4. **Schema + AI-search structure.** Every site in this set is a conventional WordPress build. None show structured data, FAQ schema, or content engineered for Google's AI Overviews. A site engineered for "Dunkin contractor Massachusetts" or "QSR remodel North Shore" as natural-language queries will outperform — especially in a market this saturated with samey copy.

5. **A real testimonial surface.** Cafco wins on this — 6+ named-operator quotes on the home page. Repro has zero. This is the easiest, highest-impact change.

## 5. Positioning statement (recommended)

> **For multi-unit QSR brands and franchisees in New England**, **Repro Construction** is the **fast-track restaurant builder** that **gets stores open faster without surprises**, because **we work the owner's side of the table — managing your money like it's ours, on a 14-day clock when you need it.**

Read against the category, this is differentiated on three vectors: vertical specificity (QSR/restaurant), speed (fast-track + the 14-day proof), and owner-side alignment (the existing voice asset Mark already uses).

## 6. Recommended messaging hierarchy for the new homepage

**Hero (above the fold):**

> **Headline:** *Fast-track restaurant builders. New store open in 14 days.*
> **Subhead:** Repro Construction has been building, remodeling, and reopening restaurants across New England for 30+ years — Dunkin', Jersey Mike's, Wayback Burgers, Dave's Hot Chicken, and more. We work the owner's side of the table.
> **CTA:** *Start your project →* (primary) and *See recent work →* (secondary)
> **Visual:** A clean, single hero image of a finished QSR interior or a time-lapse-style before/after, not a generic construction stock shot.

**Section 2 — Why operators choose Repro (the three pillars):**

| Pillar | Headline copy | Proof |
|---|---|---|
| **Speed** | "14 days. Open. Operating." | The Danvers Dunkin' case study, with photos and a 14-day timeline |
| **Owner-side** | "We manage your money like it's ours." | One pulled-out quote from Mark + a client testimonial |
| **Bench depth** | "30 years. New England top to bottom. Florida when you need us." | Client logo wall: Dunkin', Jersey Mike's, Wayback, Dave's, Smith & Wollensky, Sheriff's, Yella, Zo |

**Section 3 — What we build:**

Three tiles — **Restaurant / QSR**, **Multi-Unit & Mixed-Use**, **Residential & Custom** — each linking to a vertical landing page. Lead with QSR. The current site's flat service stack (Design-Build / CM / GC / Pre-Con) is org-chart language, not buyer language. Replace it with verticals.

**Section 4 — Recent work:**

Pull the three strongest projects (Danvers Dunkin' 14-day, Nahant St 38-unit Revere, a chef-driven or named-brand interior) with metrics: square footage, timeline, vertical. Each links to a full case study page.

**Section 5 — The team:**

Keep the warm bios that already exist (they're the warmest copy on the site today). Lead with Mark. Add real headshots if the current ones aren't strong.

**Section 6 — Testimonials:**

Three named quotes from Dunkin' / Jersey Mike's / multifamily owners. **This is the highest-leverage missing piece.** A single paragraph from a Dunkin' regional ops director is worth more than the entire current home page.

**Section 7 — Footer / contact:**

## 7. CTAs and conversion strategy

The current site offers a contact form, a phone number, and a "Free Estimate" link. That's table-stakes. Add:

- **"Start your project" primary CTA** on every page, leading to a short qualifying intake (vertical, project size, target open date, location). Routes high-fit inquiries to Mark directly.
- **"Talk to Mark" secondary CTA** — direct line / direct booking link. Plays the "named owner answers the phone" advantage.
- **Subcontractor application** — already exists, keep it. Useful for SEO and trade relationships.
- **Newsletter / project alerts** — optional, low priority.

## 10. Visual direction (high level — to be refined with design)

- **Tone:** Modern, confident, jobsite-real. Not stock-photo construction-corporate.
- **Palette:** Lean into one bold accent color. The current "Repro" wordmark is industrial; a single accent (deep red, safety yellow, or a custom blue) can anchor without restyling the mark.
- **Typography:** A modern grotesque sans (Inter, Söhne, GT America style) for headers; a workhorse text sans. Two weights, two sizes, deliberate hierarchy. Avoid Divi defaults.
- **Imagery:** Real Repro jobsite + finished-space photography only. The library is deep — use it. Avoid construction stock entirely.
- **Layout:** Big project hero modules, generous whitespace, no Divi-style "card grid of icons." One idea per screen.

## 11. What to do first (priority order)

1. **Lock the positioning** — confirm with Mark that QSR/fast-track/owner-side is the right hero promise. If he wants residential or multi-unit to lead, the headline and section order shift.
2. **Gather testimonials** — ask Mark to send 3 quotes (1 QSR, 1 multifamily, 1 residential), with permission to use names and brands where possible.
3. **Pick 3 case studies** — Danvers Dunkin' 14-day, Revere 38-unit, one chef-driven or branded restaurant. Mark sends photos, dates, square footage, project timeline.
4. **Design system kickoff** — palette + type + grid. Two-day exercise, locks the visual.
5. **Build vertical landing pages** — QSR, multi-unit, residential. Each with its own SEO target, photo set, and case study.
6. **Schema and AI-search engineering** — LocalBusiness, FAQ, named-project structured data.
7. **Launch and review** — measure inbound lead quality + organic queries in months 1–3.
