## 2. The real problem the site has to solve

Not "more leads." Not "look modern." The brief is sharper than that.

**Prospects already like Repro.** Referrals come in warm — repeat clients, franchise networks, peer networking. The leak happens at the bid stage: prospects pick a cheaper contractor.

**This means the site is a premium-justification asset, not a lead-gen funnel.** Every page should answer the unspoken question: *"why is paying Repro's number worth more than saving 8% with the cheaper guy?"*

The answer Repro can own, backed by what's already true:

1. **Brand-spec compliance.** Inspire Brands, Dunkin', Jersey Mike's, Wayback Burger all have rigid brand standards. A contractor who's done 200+ projects across this portfolio knows the specs cold. A cheaper GC learning the spec on your store costs you in change orders, re-inspections, and brand-flag rejections.
2. **Schedule certainty.** Every day a QSR is closed past target-open is rent + payroll + lost royalties hitting the franchisee. Speed and on-time delivery are dollars, not bragging rights.
3. **Single point of accountability.** Owner-side thinking is Repro's existing voice asset (*"we manage your money like it's ours"*). Cheaper GCs hand you off; Mark or Nick answers the phone.
4. **Franchisee references on demand.** Pino, Dietrich, French — three franchisee owners who've signed off. Cheaper contractors don't have this bench.

These four become the spine of the site copy. They run through hero, pillars, case studies, testimonials, FAQ.

## 4. White space Repro can own

1. **Premium-justification as the headline frame.** Nobody in the cohort addresses "why pay more" head-on. They all assume the reader has already chosen them. Repro can lead with the cost-of-cheap-GC argument and own it.
2. **Franchisee-owner voice.** Three named franchisee testimonials beats every competitor's site. Cafco wins on testimonial volume but in the wrong vertical (chef-driven, not QSR).
3. **Named owner at the front of the brand.** Mark + Nick + Chris Sibulkin (new superintendent) as named, accessible operators. Groom and Connolly use heritage language but feel institutional.
4. **GEO / AI-search engineering.** Every competitor is a conventional WordPress build. None show /llms.txt, structured data on every page, or content engineered for AI Overviews. A site built for "Dunkin contractor Massachusetts" and "Works Cafe builder New England" as natural-language queries will outperform.
5. **Speed as a measurable promise, not a vibe.** Cornerstone and Groom mention fast-track in passing. Repro can put schedule certainty in numbers (target-open vs. actual-open delta, days closed, etc.) once Mark green-lights specific metrics.
