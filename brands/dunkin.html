Dunkin' FAQ

Questions Dunkin' franchisees actually ask.

Who builds Dunkin' franchises in Massachusetts?

Repro Construction Services, headquartered at 525 Lowell St in Peabody, MA, builds and remodels Dunkin' franchises across Massachusetts and the rest of New England. Repro has delivered for named Dunkin' franchisees including Jason Pino and has standing relationships across the brand's New England operator network.

How long does a Dunkin' remodel take?

A typical Dunkin' image-refresh remodel runs eight to twelve weeks of active construction once the store is dark, depending on scope and equipment lead times. Repro plans the dark-store window against a target reopen date the franchisee hands to staffing, marketing, and the landlord, and we sign off the variance log before mobilization so the calendar doesn't slip.

What brand-spec items matter most on a Dunkin' build?

On a Dunkin', the items that fail brand audits when a non-specialist builds them are the order counter and bake case geometry, the espresso and frozen-drink line layout, drive-thru speaker post and menu board alignment, the brand-specified flooring and wall finish package, and signage mounting tolerances. Repro builds these to drawing the first time so the brand audit clears on first walk.

Where does Repro work for Dunkin' franchisees?

Massachusetts is our home market — North Shore, Greater Boston, MetroWest, the South Shore — and we serve all of New England from our Peabody office: New Hampshire, Rhode Island, Connecticut, Vermont, and Maine. Recent Dunkin' work includes 74 Middlesex Ave in Somerville, MA.

Does Repro work on Dunkin' new builds, or only remodels?

Both. Repro handles ground-up new builds, full-image remodels, kiosk and drive-thru conversions, and fast-track tenant improvements for Dunkin' franchisees. We've also worked across Dunkin's parent network — Inspire Brands is part of the Repro portfolio.
